# Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy

## Target ICP
Primary ICP: founders or engineering managers at seed to Series A SaaS companies with 5-50 employees. They approve tooling, feel runway pressure, and already pay for at least three AI tools (ChatGPT/Claude/Copilot plus one more). The buyer is budget-aware but still prioritizes shipping speed. Secondary ICP: bootstrapped agencies and small dev shops that want predictable monthly software costs.

## High-Intent Triggers
- Monthly finance close when SaaS expenses spike or show up in a larger-than-expected invoice.
- A new engineer joins and requests an AI tool seat, pushing spend over a “mental budget” threshold.
- A founder reads a thread on X about AI spend or sees a competitor ship a similar tool.
- Procurement or security review requires justification for each AI vendor.

## Distribution Channels (30-day plan)
1) **Hacker News (Show HN)**
- Post: "Show HN: A deterministic AI spend audit for startup teams (no login, instant results)."
- Include a short breakdown of the pricing database and a screenshot of a real audit result.
- Follow up in comments with why deterministic logic matters (no LLM math) and link to PRICING_DATA.

2) **Indie Hackers**
- Launch post with a transparent “build log” and a sample audit narrative.
- Reply to existing threads about runaway SaaS bills and share a free audit link.

3) **Founder Twitter/X**
- Create a 7-tweet thread using a hypothetical 8-person team and show $1,200/year in savings.
- Include a “CTA tweet” with a direct link and an image preview of the report.
- DM 20 founders per week who have recently posted about AI tools or cash burn, offering a free audit and a summary screenshot they can share.

4) **Startup Slack groups**
- Post in founder and CTO channels with a short “audit your stack” template.
- Offer to run 5 free audits manually in exchange for feedback and a quote.

5) **Reddit communities**
- Target r/startups, r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur, and r/IndieHackers with a focus on “reduce overhead” rather than “try our tool.”

## First 100 Users (specific execution)
Week 1: Launch on HN and Indie Hackers on the same day with a clear screenshot. Send 30 targeted X DMs with a short pitch and a no-ask offer: “I can run your stack and send the summary.”
Week 2: Follow up with each DM recipient, ask for two referrals, and post a second thread showing real anonymized savings.
Week 3: Publish a short blog post or Notion page with “Top 5 AI spend mistakes I saw this month” and share it in Slack communities.
Week 4: Identify 10 companies with public hiring posts mentioning AI tools; reach out directly to founders/EMs with a one-paragraph audit offer.

## Unfair Distribution Channel
Credex has real access to discounted credits and vendor relationships. That allows the audit to lead directly into a tangible solution (credits), which is stronger than generic “switch tools” advice. Competitors can copy the calculator but cannot deliver the credits.

## Week 1 Traction Targets
- 800 unique visitors
- 250 audit starts
- 120 completed audits
- 15 email captures
- 3 consultation requests

These targets are realistic for a small founder-led launch that focuses on proof of value and clear screenshots over broad marketing.
